Training programs

Structured programs designed for strength and hypertrophy.

Push pull legs (PPL) - 6 days/week

Intermediate · 12 weeks · Hypertrophy

Three alternating days split into push, pull, and leg focused sessions.

  • Push: Bench press, incline DB press, overhead press, cable flyes, tricep dips
  • Pull: Deadlifts, rows, pull-ups, lat pulldowns, face pulls
  • Legs: Squats, leg press, hamstring curls, leg extensions, calf raises

Upper lower split - 4 days/week

Beginner to intermediate · 8-12 weeks

Two upper days, two lower days. Heavy lifting twice per week per muscle group.

  • Upper A: Compound pressing, back work
  • Lower A: Squats, quad emphasis
  • Upper B: Deadlifts, rows, supplemental pulling
  • Lower B: Deadlifts, hamstring and glute emphasis

Full body 3x/week

Beginner · 12+ weeks · Strength foundation

Simple, effective routine. Each session hits all major muscle groups.

  • Compound lifts: Squat, bench, row, deadlift
  • Supplemental: Isolation for weak points

Advanced periodization - 5 days/week

Advanced · 16 weeks · Peak strength

Block periodization for lifters with 5+ years experience.

  • Phase 1: High volume (8-12 reps)
  • Phase 2: Moderate volume, higher intensity (4-8 reps)
  • Phase 3: Low volume, max effort (1-3 reps)

Weird gains tips

Bizarre but scientifically-backed methods for insane muscle growth.

01

Cold showers right after lifting KILL gains

Cold shower or ice bath within 4 hours of weight training can reduce muscle protein synthesis by up to 37%.

Science: Cold blunts the inflammatory response muscles need to grow.
02

Chewing gum makes you press more

Chewing gum before heavy sets can increase strength output by 2-3%.

Science: Jaw clench reflex recruits more motor units throughout body.
03

Heavy music = 8% more strength

Aggressive music increases testosterone response and lift performance.

Science: Adrenaline from arousing music directly impacts force production.
04

Honey before bed

Slow carbs at night fuel recovery without fat gain. Prevents cortisol spikes.

Science: Liver uses fructose to restock glycogen and keep blood sugar stable.
05

Salt your pre-workout

1/4 tsp pink salt in pre-workout water improves pumps and strength.

Science: Sodium drives water into muscle cells and improves nerve conduction.
06

Try to bend the bar

Actively pull bench press bar apart. Spread floor with feet during squats.

Science: Irradiation principle - nearby muscle contraction increases neural drive.
07

Sleep in 62F for +15% GH

Cool bedroom temps boost nighttime growth hormone release.

Science: Lower core temp extends deep sleep phases where GH is secreted.
08

Morning fasted calves

Training calves fasted activates different muscle fibers. Breaks plateaus.

Science: Fasted training hits high-threshold motor units harder.
09

Sock squats

Squatting in socks increases depth and engages more glutes.

Science: Thick shoes reduce proprioception and alter mechanics.
10

Mirror stare

Watching yourself lift improves mind-muscle connection 15-22%.

Science: Visual feedback helps brain recruit target muscles better.
11

Final set drop set

One drop set on last set triggers extra hypertrophy.

Science: Metabolic stress without overloading nervous system.
12

Ginger = -25% soreness

2g daily reduces DOMS as effectively as ibuprofen.

Science: Gingerol compounds have strong anti-inflammatory properties.
13

Legs last in your week

Heavy squats and deadlifts deplete CNS for 2-3 days.

Science: Systemic fatigue affects all other lifts.
14

Baking soda for endurance

0.3g/kg 60 min pre-workout buffers lactic acid.

Science: Neutralizes hydrogen ions that cause muscle burn.
15

Banana + PB pre-bed

Slow carbs + slow protein sustains amino acids for 7+ hours.

Science: Similar amino acid sustenance as casein at lower cost.
