Training programs

Structured programs designed for strength and hypertrophy. Pick based on your experience level and available training days.

Push pull legs (PPL) - 6 days/week

Best for: Intermediate lifters | Duration: 12 weeks | Focus: Hypertrophy

Three alternating days split into push, pull, and leg focused sessions. Higher frequency allows more volume and recovery time per muscle group.

Common exercises:

  • Push day: Bench press, incline DB press, overhead press, cable flyes, tricep dips
  • Pull day: Deadlifts, rows, pull-ups, lat pulldowns, face pulls
  • Leg day: Squats, leg press, hamstring curls, leg extensions, calf raises

Upper lower split - 4 days/week

Best for: Beginner to intermediate | Duration: 8-12 weeks | Focus: Strength and hypertrophy

Two upper body days and two lower body days. Excellent for beginners and allows heavy lifting twice per week per muscle group.

  • Upper A: Compound pressing, supplemental pressing, back work
  • Upper B: Deadlifts, rows, supplemental pulling
  • Lower A: Squats, quad emphasis
  • Lower B: Deadlifts, hamstring and glute emphasis

Full body 3x/week

Best for: Beginner | Duration: 12+ weeks | Focus: Strength foundation

Simple, effective routine. Each session hits all major muscle groups. Perfect for building base strength before specializing.

  • Compound lifts: Squat, bench, row, deadlift
  • Supplemental: Isolation for weak points
  • Rest days: Important for recovery

Advanced periodization - 5 days/week

Best for: Advanced | Duration: 16 weeks | Focus: Peak strength

Block periodization with accumulation, intensification, and realization phases. For lifters with 5+ years experience targeting specific peak performance.

  • Phase 1: High volume (8-12 reps)
  • Phase 2: Moderate volume, higher intensity (4-8 reps)
  • Phase 3: Low volume, max effort (1-3 reps)

Nutrition guides

Bulking phase

Eat in a 500 calorie surplus above maintenance. Aim for 0.8-1g protein per lb bodyweight. Prioritize dense carbs (rice, pasta, oats) for energy and volume.

Duration: 4-16 weeks depending on starting point and goals.

Cutting phase

Eat in a 500 calorie deficit. Maintain high protein (1-1.2g per lb) to preserve muscle. Reduce carbs slightly, keep fat moderate (0.3g per lb).

Duration: 8-16 weeks depending on how lean you want to get.

Meal timing fundamentals

Post-workout: Fast-digesting carbs + protein within 2 hours. Pre-workout: Moderate carbs + protein 2-3 hours before. Daily adherence to total macros matters more than timing.

Consistent nutrition beats perfect meal timing every time.

Recovery optimization

Sleep protocols

Sleep is where muscle is built. Aim for 7-9 hours nightly. Keep bedroom cool (65-68°F), dark, and quiet. Consistent sleep schedule is more important than duration.

Strategy: Same bedtime and wake time every day. Avoid screens 1 hour before bed. No caffeine after 2pm.

Stretching and mobility

10-15 minutes daily improves recovery and prevents injury. Focus on tight areas: hip flexors, hamstrings, thoracic spine, shoulders.

Science: Static stretching post-workout (30 sec/muscle). Dynamic stretching pre-workout. Consistency matters more than duration.

Foam rolling

30 seconds per muscle group, 2-3 times per week. Calves, quads, IT band, glutes, lats. Can be done post-workout or on rest days.

Evidence: Reduces soreness, improves blood flow, increases ROM. Not a replacement for stretching.

Deload weeks

Every 4-6 weeks, reduce volume by 40-50% and intensity slightly. Still train, but lighter. Crucial for long-term progress and preventing burnout.

Benefits: Allows CNS recovery, reduces injury risk, lets connective tissue adapt.

Hydration

Aim for 0.5-1 oz per lb bodyweight daily. More if training hard or in hot environment. Electrolytes matter for performance and recovery.

Tip: Drink with meals, sip throughout the day. Dark urine = dehydrated. Pale yellow = adequate.

Stress management

Chronic stress elevates cortisol, killing gains. Meditate 10 minutes daily, take walks, maintain social connections, manage work stress.

Research: Stress management improves strength gains by 5-10%. Sleep quality is the easiest leverage point.
