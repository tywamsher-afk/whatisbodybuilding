Training programs

Structured programs designed for strength and hypertrophy. Pick based on your experience level and available training days.

Push pull legs (PPL) - 6 days/week

Intermediate · 12 weeks · Hypertrophy

Three alternating days split into push, pull, and leg focused sessions. Higher frequency allows more volume per muscle group.

  • Push: Bench press, incline DB press, overhead press, cable flyes, tricep dips
  • Pull: Deadlifts, rows, pull-ups, lat pulldowns, face pulls
  • Legs: Squats, leg press, hamstring curls, leg extensions, calf raises

Upper lower split - 4 days/week

Beginner to intermediate · 8-12 weeks · Strength & hypertrophy

Two upper days, two lower days. Allows heavy lifting twice per week per muscle group.

  • Upper A: Compound pressing, supplemental pressing, back work
  • Upper B: Deadlifts, rows, supplemental pulling
  • Lower A: Squats, quad emphasis
  • Lower B: Deadlifts, hamstring and glute emphasis

Full body 3x/week

Beginner · 12+ weeks · Strength foundation

Simple, effective routine. Each session hits all major muscle groups.

  • Compound lifts: Squat, bench, row, deadlift
  • Supplemental: Isolation for weak points

Advanced periodization - 5 days/week

Advanced · 16 weeks · Peak strength

Block periodization with accumulation, intensification, and realization phases. For lifters with 5+ years experience.

  • Phase 1: High volume (8-12 reps)
  • Phase 2: Moderate volume, higher intensity (4-8 reps)
  • Phase 3: Low volume, max effort (1-3 reps)

Nutrition guides

Bulking

Eat in a 500 calorie surplus. Aim for 0.8-1g protein per lb bodyweight. Prioritize dense carbs for energy.

Cutting

Eat in a 500 calorie deficit. Maintain high protein (1-1.2g per lb). Reduce carbs, keep fat moderate.

Meal timing

Post-workout: Fast carbs + protein within 2 hrs. Daily adherence matters more than timing.

Weird gains tips

Bizarre but scientifically-backed methods for insane muscle growth.

01

Cold showers right after lifting KILL gains

Taking a cold shower or ice bath within 4 hours of weight training can reduce muscle protein synthesis by up to 37%.

Science: Cold blunts the inflammatory response your muscles need to grow. Save the cold plunge for non-training days.
02

Chewing gum makes you press more weight

Chewing gum before heavy sets can increase strength output by 2-3% by activating the masseter muscle, which is neurally linked to grip and core activation.

Science: The jaw clench reflex recruits more motor units throughout the body.
03

Listen to heavy music for 8% more strength

Aggressive music (metal, rap, hardstyle) increases testosterone response and lift performance compared to calm music or silence.

Science: Adrenaline release from arousing music directly impacts force production.
04

Eat a spoonful of honey before bed

Slow-digesting carbs at night fuel overnight recovery. Honey specifically prevents the overnight cortisol spike that kills gains.

Science: The liver uses fructose to restock glycogen and keep blood sugar stable during sleep.
05

Salt your pre-workout drink

Adding 1/4 tsp of pink salt to your pre-workout water improves pumps, strength, and endurance.

Science: Sodium drives water into muscle cells and improves nerve conduction.
06

Grip the bar with intent to bend it

When benching, actively try to pull the bar apart. When squatting, spread the floor with your feet.

Science: Irradiation principle - contracting nearby muscles hard increases neural drive to target muscles.
07

Sleep in a 62-degree room for +15% growth hormone

Cooler bedroom temps significantly boost nighttime growth hormone release.

Science: Lower core body temp extends deep sleep phases where GH is secreted.
08

Do calf raises first thing in the morning

Training calves fasted in the morning activates different muscle fibers and breaks plateaus.

Science: Fasted training hits high-threshold motor units harder. Calves respond to frequency.
09

The sock squat technique

Squatting in just socks can increase depth, engage more glutes, and build better ankle mobility.

Science: Thick-soled shoes reduce proprioception and alter squat mechanics.
10

Staring at yourself in the mirror

Watching yourself lift improves mind-muscle connection by 15-22%, measured by EMG.

Science: Visual feedback helps your brain recruit target muscles more effectively.
11

Drop sets on last set of every exercise

One drop set at the end of your last set triggers extra hypertrophy with minimal recovery cost.

Science: Additional metabolic stress without overloading the nervous system.
12

Ginger tea reduces muscle soreness 25%

2g of ginger daily reduces delayed-onset muscle soreness as effectively as ibuprofen.

Science: Gingerol compounds have strong anti-inflammatory properties.
13

Train legs last in your weekly split

Save legs for the last training day. Heavy squats and deadlifts deplete your CNS.

Science: Systemic fatigue from leg day affects all other lifts for 2-3 days.
14

Baking soda before high rep sets

0.3g/kg of sodium bicarbonate 60 min pre-workout buffers lactic acid for 2-3 extra reps.

Science: Sodium bicarbonate neutralizes hydrogen ions that cause muscle burn.
15

Eat a banana with peanut butter pre-bed

Slow carbs (banana) + slow protein/fat (PB) sustains amino acids for 7+ hours of overnight growth.

Science: Similar amino acid sustenance as casein at much lower cost.

Recovery

Optimize sleep, mobility, and recovery to maximize gains.

Sleep protocols

Sleep is where muscle is built. Aim for 7-9 hours nightly. Keep bedroom cool (62F), dark, and quiet.

Stretching & mobility

10-15 minutes daily improves recovery and prevents injury. Focus on hip flexors, hamstrings, shoulders.

Foam rolling

30 seconds per muscle group, 2-3 times per week. Calves, quads, IT band, glutes, lats.

Deload weeks

Every 4-6 weeks, reduce volume by 40-50%. Still train, but lighter. Crucial for long-term progress.

Hydration

Aim for 0.5-1 oz per lb bodyweight daily. Electrolytes matter for performance and recovery.

Stress management

Chronic stress elevates cortisol, killing gains. Meditate 10 minutes daily, maintain social connections.
